## Key Notes

### Driving Information
- **Left-hand driving countries** (mostly former British colonies): Antigua and Barbuda, The Bahamas, Barbados, Bermuda, British Virgin Islands, Cayman Islands, Dominica, Grenada, Guyana, Jamaica, Montserrat, Saint Kitts and Nevis, Saint Lucia, Saint Vincent and the Grenadines, Suriname, Trinidad and Tobago, Turks and Caicos Islands, U.S. Virgin Islands (unique!)
- **Right-hand driving countries**: All others (Cuba, Haiti, Dominican Republic, Puerto Rico, French territories, Dutch territories, Belize)

### Canadian Visa Policies
- **Visa-free access** is granted to most independent Caribbean nations and British/Dutch territories
- **Special cases**:
  - Saint Lucia and Saint Vincent: Visa required but may be eligible for eTA if previously held Canadian visa or have valid US non-immigrant visa
  - Cuba requires a Tourist Card (not a traditional visa)
  - Suriname requires e-Visa or Tourist Card
  - French territories follow separate rules from Schengen (90-day visa-free stays)

### Nigerian Visa Policies
- **Visa-free countries** (limited): Barbados, Dominica, Grenada, Guyana, Saint Kitts and Nevis, Saint Vincent and the Grenadines, Trinidad and Tobago
- **eVisa/VoA available**: Antigua and Barbuda (with conditions), The Bahamas
- **Visa required**: Most other territories, including all French, Dutch, British, and U.S. territories

### Important Reminders
- Always verify current requirements before travel as visa policies can change
- Passport validity of at least 6 months is typically required
- Proof of onward travel and sufficient funds may be required upon entry
- Some territories require specific health documentation (e.g., yellow fever vaccination for French Guiana)
